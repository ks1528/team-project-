Lessons

Lesson 1: Ratios

Ratios compare quantities. They help us understand relationships between numbers.

Example: If there are 2 apples and 4 oranges, the ratio is 2:4.

Ratios can be simplified just like fractions. Divide both numbers by the same number.

Lesson 2: Fractions

Fractions represent parts of a whole.

The top number is called the numerator and the bottom number is the denominator.

Fractions can be added, subtracted, multiplied and divided.

Lesson 3: Decimals

Decimals are another way to represent fractions.

They are used frequently in money, measurements and science.

Understanding place value helps you work with decimals easily.

Lesson 4: BIDMAS

BIDMAS helps us solve calculations in the correct order.

If we solve calculations in the wrong order we may get incorrect answers.

This rule ensures everyone solving the problem gets the same answer.
