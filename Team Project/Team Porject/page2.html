Math Lessons

Ratios

Ratios compare quantities. For example, 2:4 means for every 2 of one thing there are 4 of another. Simplifying ratios makes them easier to understand.

Try this: If a recipe uses 2 cups of flour and 4 cups of sugar, the ratio is 2:4 or simplified 1:2.

Fractions

Fractions represent parts of a whole. Learn to add, subtract, multiply, and divide fractions carefully.

Example: 1/2 + 1/2 = 1, 3/4 + 1/2 = 5/4

Decimals

Decimals are another way of representing parts of a whole. For instance, 0.5 = 1/2. Practice converting fractions to decimals and vice versa.

BIDMAS

BIDMAS stands for Brackets, Indices, Division/Multiplication, Addition, Subtraction. Follow this order to solve complex equations correctly.

Example: 2 + 3 × 4 = 14 (do multiplication first).

Tips & Tricks

  • Always simplify ratios and fractions before using them.
  • Check your decimal calculations with fractions for accuracy.
  • Remember BIDMAS order – brackets first, then indices, etc.
  • Practice makes perfect – try a small quiz after each lesson!

Math Fun Facts

  • Did you know? Zero is the only number that cannot be represented in Roman numerals.
  • Fractions were used in Ancient Egypt over 4,000 years ago!
  • The symbol for division (÷) is called an obelus.
  • Pi (π) is a never-ending, never-repeating number.
